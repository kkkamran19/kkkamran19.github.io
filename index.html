A little about me

Hello there! I’m Kamran Khan, a passionate Hardware Engineer with a Master’s Degree in Micro and Nanotechnology from TU Ilmenau in Germany. My core interests lie in embedded systems, FPGA development, and signal processing especially in building intelligent neuromorphic hardware. I’ve worked on adaptive feedback architectures, real-time acoustic sensing, and FPGA-based mixed signal system design, and I’m always exploring how digital logic can solve complex, real-world problems.

Beyond the technical side, I enjoy documenting my work and making engineering more accessible through clear writing and research. I also like working out, listening to music, and diving into retrocomputing and vintage electronics, especially anything from the 90's. Whether it's a new design challenge or an old circuit to revive, I’m always curious and ready to explore.
